    Bibliography¶

    Sanitised, publishable reference library for the research publication set. The internal workspace keeps working annotations and system-specific notes separately.

    Multi-agent systems and agentic workflows¶

    • Wei, J., et al. (2022). Chain-of-thought prompting elicits reasoning in large language models. NeurIPS 2022. — Reasoning decomposition; relevant to how agents break down multi-step operational tasks.
    • Wang, L., et al. (2024). A survey on large language model based autonomous agents. Frontiers of Computer Science. — General survey of architectures and capabilities; provides context for role specialisation.
    • Xi, Z., et al. (2023). The rise and potential of large language model based agents: A survey. arXiv:2309.07864. — Surveys planning, memory, and tool use; motivates the memory and dispatch concerns in the field study.
    • Park, J. S., et al. (2023). Generative agents: Interactive simulacra of human behaviour. ACM UIST 2023. — Memory-stream architecture for long-running agents; compare to the externalised-session-state pattern.

    Autonomous operations and SRE¶

    • STRATUS: Autonomous Cloud Reliability Engineering (IBM Research, arXiv:2506.02009, NeurIPS 2025). — Closest parallel to this work: an LLM-based multi-agent system for autonomous cloud reliability engineering. Cited to position the present paper as complementary (patterns and lessons) rather than competing.
    • Beyer, B., et al. (2016). Site Reliability Engineering: How Google Runs Production Systems. O'Reilly. — Source of gates, ownership, post-mortems, and error budgets; the SRE lineage in which the observed patterns sit.
    • Gawande, A. (2009). The Checklist Manifesto: How to Get Things Right. Metropolitan Books. — Checklist and ritual as coordination tools; relevant to verifiable dispatch and landing rituals.
    • Limoncelli, T. A., et al. (2014). The Practice of Cloud System Administration. Addison-Wesley. — Operational patterns for distributed systems; background for build/deploy/runtime sections.
    • Allspaw, J. (2015). The Field Guide to Understanding 'Human Error'. 3rd ed. Productivity Press. — Incident archetype framing; helps justify why interaction patterns matter more than root-cause labels.
    • Dekker, S. W. A. (2011). Drift into Failure: From Hunting Broken Components to Understanding Complex Systems. Ashgate. — Drift and erosion of safety constraints; directly relevant to the drifting-context anti-pattern.

    Memory for agents¶

    • Park et al., 2023 (above) — Generative Agents memory stream: reflection, retrieval, importance scoring.
    • MemGPT / Letta, Zep — Persistent memory backends for conversational agents. Compare as product-layer memory systems, not as deployment infrastructure.
    • Open Knowledge Format (OKF) v0.1 — Google. File-format layer for portable structured knowledge; compare at format layer, not runtime layer.

    Evaluation¶

    • Hendrycks, D., et al. (2021). Measuring massive multitask language understanding. ICLR 2021. — General LLM capability benchmark; contrast with fleet-level collaboration metrics.
    • Liu, X., et al. (2023). AgentBench: Evaluating LLMs as agents. ICLR 2024. — Multi-task agent benchmark; relevant to Paper 5 (evaluation protocol).
    • Jimenez, C., et al. (2023). SWE-bench: Can language models resolve real-world GitHub issues? NeurIPS 2023 Datasets and Benchmarks. — End-to-end software-engineering evaluation; cited in §5.2 on metric calibration and end-to-end gates.
    • Zheng, L., et al. (2023). Judging LLM-as-a-judge with MT-bench and chatbot arena. NeurIPS 2023. — LLM-as-a-judge methods; relevant for evaluating agent outputs in Paper 5.

    Agent coordination frameworks and role models¶

    • AutoGen / Multi-Agent Conversation Framework (Microsoft Research). — General conversation-based multi-agent framework; compare to the role-routing and dispatch patterns.
    • CrewAI — Role-based agent crews. — Directly comparable to the role taxonomy; note that CrewAI roles are often task-centric rather than operational.
    • DSPy — Declarative language model programming and optimisers. — Declarative program construction; compare to the explicit-contracts argument in the Discussion.

    Practitioner engineering¶

    • Nygard, M. T. (2018). Release It! Design and Deploy Production-Ready Software. 2nd ed. Pragmatic Bookshelf. — Stability patterns and deployment anti-patterns; background for §6.
